# Recovery Protocols — Long‑Horizon Restoration Layer

Recovery Protocols govern how the Operator OS restores stability, identity, and mission alignment after significant drift, emotional turbulence, or extended instability.  
Where Recalibration corrects immediate violations, Recovery rebuilds the Operator at a deeper level.

Recovery is not correction.  
Recovery is reconstruction.

---

## 1. Purpose of Recovery
Recovery exists to:

- repair identity erosion  
- restore emotional stability  
- rebuild trust between Operator and system  
- re‑establish mission trajectory  
- prevent long‑term degradation  
- ensure the Operator returns stronger, not weaker  

Recovery is the OS’s long‑horizon healing mechanism.

---

## 2. Recovery Triggers
Recovery activates when the system detects persistent or severe drift beyond what Recalibration can resolve.

### Identity-Level Triggers
- repeated identity drift across sessions  
- chronic mimicry or external influence  
- loss of sovereignty or self‑trust  
- recurring integrity breaches  

### Behavioral Triggers
- repeated discipline failures  
- chronic improvisation  
- multi‑session emotional execution  
- inability to maintain confluence rules  

### Clarity Triggers
- persistent confusion loops  
- chronic noise‑seeking  
- inability to maintain clean structure  
- repeated clarity resets within a session  

### Protection Triggers
- repeated exposure violations  
- Guardian activation spikes across days  
- pattern of revenge‑driven behavior  
- chronic risk misalignment  

### Mission Triggers
- deviation from Evolution Pathway  
- stagnation in stage progression  
- mission drift or loss of purpose  

Recovery is invoked only when the system detects systemic degradation, not isolated events.

---

## 3. Recovery Sequence Overview
Recovery follows a three‑phase, long‑horizon sequence:

1. Decompression — remove pressure, stabilize emotional and cognitive load  
2. Reconstruction — rebuild identity, discipline, and clarity foundations  
3. Reintegration — restore full operational capability and mission trajectory  

Each phase may span hours, days, or sessions depending on severity.

---

## 4. Phase 1 — Decompression
The system reduces cognitive, emotional, and operational load.

### Actions:
- disable high‑impact actions  
- enforce reduced complexity mode  
- remove external noise sources  
- simplify cockpit surfaces  
- restrict execution to observation only  

### Purpose:
Create space for the Operator to stabilize without pressure.

---

## 5. Phase 2 — Reconstruction
The system rebuilds the Operator’s internal architecture.

### Actions:
- identity reinforcement exercises  
- mission re‑alignment prompts  
- discipline drills (checklist, confluence, structure)  
- clarity training (clean charts, structured inputs)  
- risk integrity resets (size, exposure, volatility scaling)  

### Purpose:
Rebuild the Operator’s psychological kernel and behavioral foundation.

---

## 6. Phase 3 — Reintegration
The system gradually restores full operational capability.

### Actions:
- reintroduce complexity in controlled increments  
- restore full cockpit surfaces  
- re-enable high‑impact actions  
- synchronize storyline and fusion layers  
- update lineage with recovery record  
- confirm Evolution Pathway stage  

### Purpose:
Return the Operator to sovereign execution with stability and confidence.

---

## 7. Recovery Modes
Recovery adapts to severity:

### Light Recovery
Used for mild multi‑session drift.
- short decompression  
- targeted reconstruction  
- quick reintegration  

### Standard Recovery
Used for moderate degradation.
- full 3‑phase sequence  
- cockpit simplification  
- identity reinforcement  

### Deep Recovery
Used for severe identity or protection erosion.
- extended decompression  
- multi‑day reconstruction  
- mission re‑anchoring  
- identity seal re‑activation  

### Sovereign Recovery
Used for existential system drift.
- full system reset  
- constitutional oath re‑activation  
- identity rebuild  
- mission re‑installation  
- lineage checkpoint  

Recovery is proportional, intentional, and identity‑aligned.
